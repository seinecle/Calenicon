Calenicon

(disclaimer: this app is in early beta!)

NESSHI is a European research project involving scientists from the social science, the humanities and the neuroscience.

One of NESSHI's goals is to study empirically the growing influence of neuroscience in society. To this end, one sub projects of NESSHI consisted in tracing the presence of "neuro-objects" (the brain, neuroimaging devices...) in popular media.

So far, 2 Dutch newspapers have been sampled and photographed over a period covering 1987-2011.

We collected so far about 1,500 pictures and plan for a possible extension of the data collection effort. But we already need to plan for the most adequate way to inspect, share, edit, and preserve the data to the best standards.

It appeared that no off-the-shelves solution exist for these specifications. We conducted an attentive watch of the recent developments in the open data and linked data movements to find the building blocks that would meet our needs.

1. OPEN ACCESS, OPEN DATA: The creation of open repositories is one of the notable recent achievements of these movements.

Figshare, Dataverse and CKAN are three platforms providing free and permanent, and high standard hosting of datasets for scholarly research.

We chose CKAN primarily because it offers the permanent referencing of individual resources, not just the full dataset.

In our case, this feature is particularly important as we need to cite and refer to individual pictures, not just to the full album of photographs.

2. METADATA, LINKED DATA: VRACore is a joint initiative by Adobe, the Library of Congress to develop a "data standard for the description of works of visual culture as well as the images that document them".

We use VRACore to describe our visuals as it presents a perfect opportunity to standardize our metadata, making it easier to re-use our dataset by anyone for future research
